Hi, I'm Jon.

I explore, analyze, and build.

I explore and seek knowledge

I am currently working on my PhD in Chemical & Biological Engineering at Northwestern University under the guidance of Keith Tyo and Linda Broadbelt. My overarching research objective is to use computational tools to explore and simulate biological systems to help create useful chemicals for society in an environmentally sustainable way.

I have always been fascinated by biology, and my current projects focus on:

  • Discovering novel, useful chemicals in bacteria that can consume waste products
  • Simulating complex biological systems
  • Quantitatively characterizing a DNA-based biological recording system

I analyze and interpret data

I love delving into data and finding hidden patterns. I have experience with machine learning, developing mathematical models, as well as doing both frequentist and Bayesian statistical analyses. The python data science stack is my bread and butter.

I build useful software tools

I enjoy the development process, and I try to build tools that are useful for others. I have built web APIs and small websites using flask, although I built this site using Jekyll. I help maintain the MINE database website which is a highly cited research tool for discovering new chemical compounds. Finally, I am excited to be working on my first iOS app. It's an app for Japanese language learners (like myself) where you solve crosswords while improving your Japanese reading skills!
